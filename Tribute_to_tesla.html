The birth of a Genius

Nikola Tesla was born during a lightning storm on the night of July 9–10, 1856, in the village of Smiljan,
then part of the Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). According to legend, as Tesla's mother gave birth, a flash of lightning struck,
prompting the midwife to say he would be a "child of darkness."
" His mother, however, responded, "No, he will be a child of light." This dramatic birth foreshadowed Tesla's future as a brilliant inventor who revolutionized electrical engineering.

Timeline of Nikola Tesla

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire.
  • 1884 - Moved to the U.S. and worked with Edison.
  • 1888 - Developed the AC motor.
  • 1891 - Invented the Tesla Coil.
  • 1893 - Demonstrated wireless power at the Chicago World’s Fair.
  • 1943 - Passed away, leaving a legacy of innovation.
